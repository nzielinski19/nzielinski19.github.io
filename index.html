Voting in Virginia

Does Frequency of Voting Change With Income and Education Levels?

This plot uses fiveThirtyeight voting data from Virginia that comes from the article "Why Many Americans Don't Vote."
This data explores the characteristics and beliefs of voters and non-voters in US elections. In this specific plot, we
will look at self-reported education levels, income levels, and frequency with which the respondents vote.

We can see from this plot that around half of people who self-reported that they never vote have less that a high school
education. On the other hand, around half of self-reported "always" voters have a college education. Sporadic is a relatively
even spread in terms of education level. There does not seem to be a strong correlation between income level and voting frequency.

How Did Voter Turnout and Absentee Voting Change in Virginia General Elections from 2014 to 2022?

This plot uses Virginia voting data over the years 2014-2022. It shows the relationship between absentee voters and voter turnout by region.
A tooltip is included to show each county data. It is important to note that in the original data, some voter turnout
percentages were above 100%. These values are included in the final data to ensure the visualization is accurate. However, the values
greater than 100% are not visible on the plot.

Notice in the animation that the percentage of absentee voters in 2020 was much higher that other years. This is shown by bubbles
much higher on the y axis in the year 2020. There are many factors that influence absentee voting, but we can assume that COVID-19 played
a large part in this difference in numbers in 2020. The 2020 election occurred at the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic, causing many voters to
mail in their ballots. There are also high voter turnouts due to the fact 2020 was a highly contentious presidential election year. Notice that
both 2016 and 2020 there is a jump in voter turnout percent. This is because both of these years were presidential elections. This plot also
reveals that the Northern and Hampton Roads regions of Virginia consistently have the largest voter registration. We might attribute this to
Richmond and DC suburbs being located in these regions, which have high population concentrations.

What Did Voter Turnout Look Like in Cities and Counties in Virginia in the 2022 General Election?

The choropleth map of voter turnout by county in Virginia reveals that in 2022, there were relatively lower voter turnout rates in
Southwestern Virginia. We also see that near Washington DC in the North and Richmond near the Eastern Coast have higher turnouts.
In the middle of the state there seems to be average voter turnout rates between 40% and 56%.
